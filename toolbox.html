Art, Design, and Marketing

Aggie.io
Impressive considering where programs started. Aggie.io is a web-based collaborative drawing program that's available browsers.
Canva
Canva is a free multi-platform tool for quick drag-and-drop graphic design and social media marketing. Premium features for Non-Profits, which includes their campaign planner feature, and Education accounts are available for free as well.
Clip Studio Paint
My personal favorite drawing program.
Glimpse
For digital illustration, pixel art, and animation. A better alternative to its original fork.
Font Forge
Open source font maker. No restrictions on characters compares to the free version of Caligraphr.
Krita
My personal favorite free desktop drawing program, also it's open source.
MailChimp
Among my least favorite tools to use. While it is among the most popular for making "pretty" newsletters for free, MailChimp is actually a general marketing tool that can plan out more than just newsletters. Don't get easily swayed to thinking that the paid version is a must have, there are plenty of work arounds when you're just starting out to do what you need.
Medibang
A multi-platform supportive drawing program that as a solid free version. which means you can get used to just one UI no matter what device you're on. It is my preferred drawing app for that reason.
PiktoChart
Piktochart is an easy to use drag-and-drop infographic maker optimized for displaying data with concise graphics including spreadsheet import support, thus making it more robust for formatting quantitative informating than Canva.
SumoPaint
SumoPaint was one of the first free drawing programs I ever used because their program is optimized to be used on your browser. While I wouldn't consider it among the best of its kind, it's definitely a solid contender for learning how to get used to digital illustration tools if you're a beginner.

Planning and Time Management

Calendly
No more email threads about trying to plan the perfect time for your clients to meet with you with just one link. Schedule meetings on your own time giving them only ones that you say are available and limit the number of client interactions per week.
Doodle
Similar to Calendly, but better for getting a broad selection of possible dates based on the whole team's availability.
Plan
The #1 Planner I'll swear by after being a user for over six years Combine your to-do list, project timelines, personal calendars, team assignments, and more all in one platform easily accessible from mobile or browsers. Supports ton of different integrations including Google Calendar and Google Drive to have everything you need for accomplishing your planning everything a breeze.
Forest
Gamify going offline. Forest lets you plant trees as you get off your phone or your browser for a set duration. Customize your experience to limit your access to only specific apps and sites.
StayFocusd
Stay focused with StayFocusd by blocking you access to websites by setting certain access hours or a total amount. Great for getting yourself to build up habits to become less dependent on social media sites. While the extension now seems to be developed out under Freedom which offers a broader variety of features to make itself a competitor to Cold Turkey, the original extension still appears to be live.

Programming and Development

CodePen
Built, test, present, collaborate, and discover open source front-end code. A great place to learn and test HTML, (S)CSS, and JavaScript simultaneously with a super friendly and supportive community.
Codeshare
Collaborative coding without all the fluff. Quickly get a link to a text editor to share with others while being able to set the Syntax and Keymap with a few clicks. Great for drafting or dumping code with teams.
MWeb
An easy, no nonsense, and free-to-use markdown editor that supports cloud storage. I use this when I need to edit files from my Obsidian on the go. It obviously won't support backlinks, since everything is written in markdown, nothing will be lost no matter what platform the file is accessed on.
Scratch
Scratch is a visual programming language that utilizes a drag-and-drop interface to introduce beginners to programming. The web-based platform also offers the opportunity to enage with other learners of Scratch and is meant to be very kid friendly.

Project Management and Creative Ideation

Mentimeter
Make clean and interactive presentations with ease. Great for running live polls, quizzes, word clouds, Q&As â€” all on just ONE presentation platform. Also has a number of integration support for other presentation platforms such as Miro and Brella. Mentimeter is an experience in and of itself and is EASILY a meeting and classroom game-changer.
Miro
A collaborative whiteboard for brainstorming, strategy planning, and idea mapping. My latest favorite. Plus, it's great for agile workflows or presentations while supporting a HUGE amount of integrations. My definite go-to for creating creative and interactive experiences.
Monday
Not your average project management spreadsheet. Build up your workflow using Monday to assign and comment on tasks, view timelines or kanban boards, and power-up with plenty of integrations. Check-in every Monday seeing what everyone is up to while knowing exactly what YOU need to do.
Notion
Notion is a workspace tool that can be used in TONS of different ways. Whether its for teaching a class and needing documents that can be edited live, or managing a personal project where you need to be able to connect documents back to your tasks and overall roadmap, Notion does it all in one clean and easy to use platform. Display your documentation exactly how you want to and toggle fluidly between views.
Padlet
Padlet is a versatile and collaborative platform to communicate information through multi-media maps, timelines, canvases, and more. Great for hosting projects in and of itself, Padlet is definitely a platform worth introducing to your students when thinking about the various ways to tell stories or present collections.
Trello
The platform that made kanban view mainstream. Great for teams or personal use, Trello makes the most of the kanban experience by making each card so much more than just a card. Trello was personally especially useful for me as a web developer to recieve tickets for bugs and improvements from my client easily. Trello was recently acquired by Atlassian which also develops another well known software development tool for agile teams known as Jira.

Utilities

Beeline Reader
A desktop extension and pdf reader that creates a gradient over the text displayed, improving the readability.
Brave
Faster, more secure, and lighter than Chrome â€” but still built on Chromium plus Tor support. Interestingly enough, they also have a rewards system for content creators, though it requires you to create an Uphold account which asks for your financial info.
Dark Sky
The Dark Sky Company originally launched the web version of their services on Forecast.io until they decided to push DarkSky.net to be better optimzed with the features they'd built for the mobile application. Apple recently acquired Dark Sky which meant in a few years restrictions for the API would be rolled out. Personally, I can't imagine using any other weather app, with a clean UI that allows you to see weather patterns over time and see accurate precitipation predictions unlike what I've seen anywhere else, I hope some version of Dark Sky is preserved for the whole internet without a paywall.
Duet
Turn your tablet into a second monitor.
Espanso
Tired of going on Google to copy paste an em dash every time you want one? A cross-platform text expander written in rust that quickly replaces keywords into symbols, dates, emojis, and even code.
Flux
It's hard to tell if blue light glasses, you know... work. So instead of making that investment, tune it down from your screen itself. Flux adjusts the tint of your screen based on the time of day. Just remember to turn it off if you're doing color sensitive work into the evening hours â€” I say as an illustrator who realizes too late that I'm working on the wrong palette.
Line Dictionary
Ncikuused to be my favorite Chinese-English dictionary. However, around 2014, it was shut down and set to redirect to Line Dictionary. Many of the features had to be rebuilt from the wealth that once was the original platform, the UX is still among my favorite for Chinese-English dictionaries on desktop.
Loom
Loom lets you quickly sharescreen recordings either utilizng a desktop or chrome extension. If you're familar with Gyazo, I'd say Loom is somewhat like that in its quick url generating capabilites for your screenshots, but even better optimized for screen recordings, making it especially useful for working with clients by sending screenrecordings with voice-overs when reporting bugs and feature updates with a few clicks of a button and giving them an easy link to access the clip from. It supports up to 1080p for its free version, which is impressive since that's pretty high quality for generally pretty short clips!
MAL-Sync
Never worry about manually updating your animanga (anime and manga) list again. MAL-Sync supports MyAnimeList, AniList, Kitsu, and Simkl integration, automatically updating your animanga tracking per episode or chapter and also giving you recommendations on sites where you can watch or read the series you're looking for.
Mathpix Snip
This is more than your typical OCR scanner. With automatic LaTeX support, screenshot formulas of your textbook or the web and convert them into LaTeX for your preferred documentation. While it also has features like a solver and some mixed results when trying to test it on the worst of math professors handwritting that make it a bit more questionable as a service, for a curious mind, the Stack Exchange search integration is super interesting to find related threads on specific formulatic topics. You can get an extra 50 snips (or 200 with an .edu email) if you sign up using my referral link here.
GoodNotes
My personal favorite handwriting tool. Go paperless and take handwritten notes on the iPad. This app includes searchable handwriting recognition, local or cloud syncing, spaced-based reptition flash card creation, split screen support, and more.
One-Time Secret
Share information with a quick link that allows you to send text, such as passwords and other personal information, in a more secure means than texting or emailing it to someone. One someone opens the link, the text file contents is immediately deleted from the servers â€” self destructing!
Podcastle
Podcastle is a screenreader optimized to convert articles into audio clips on your browser. Compared to other t2s programs, they use their own machine learning algorithmn to make the audio sound more natural to how it would be spoken. Another great feature is that after converting an article, you can then share that audio clip with others or embed it.
Pleco
The best Chinese dictionary app with must-have features for learners such as flashcards and OCR. Its dictionary sources are constantly expanding and it's been adapted for a number of variants including Taiwanese, Mandarin, Classical and Cantonese, with both Traditional and Simplified support.
Signal
The only blue text bubble anyone should really care about. Better private multi-media texting and calling. Even supports groups. Built open source.
Spotify Lyrics
This desktop app detects what song you're listening to on Spotify and automaticaly fetches and displays the lyrics for that song from a set database of lyrics sites. Useful if you want to karaoke your way through a work session.
Xodo
Though not my favorite tool, Xodo gets the job done better than a free version of Adobe products or whatnot. The best part of Xodo is the abilitity to comment on highlights and other markdown annotations with addtional thoughts. With collaborative sharing features, this can lead to conversations. However, it's Google Drive support has been bugging lately, so while it generally has great app support, not being able to rely on cloud storage for syncing has been a bit of a thorn of my side. Though workarounds can be found, it's far from perfect.
Zgenda
Wait wait wait... what are we talking about right now? Zgenda utilizes the captions feature of Zoom to broadcast your meeting agenda so that people know what you're discussing and how long you'll be doing it for. A simple and smart tool, though maybe not all it's cracked up to be, it's one less task for a moderator to worry about â€” assuming everyone knows how to utilize closed captions and will actually end their discussions on time.
Zotero
Another tool I'll swear by for sure: Zotero makes organizing research, from preserving documents, to making highly adaptable bibliographies, and making your citations workflow a breeze. As an open source tool, tons of extensions that expand the features for a curated researcher's need, such as Zotfile. It's Google Chrome and Google Doc extensions are also super easy to use.

Art, Design, and Marketing

Drawpile
A collaborative drawing program. The desktop application allows you to host canvases, manage users, and record sessions.
Figma
Collaboratively create clean and professional vector graphic, UI, and UX designs for marketing, apps, and sites.
FireAlpaca
A free drawing program.
Hexels
Hexels 2 originally caught by eye as a program optimized for trixel atwork, notably for its ability to render isometric designs and illustrations. Since then, the newest version of the program, Hexels 3, has expanded its features to be optimized well for both high quality pixel and trixel art, but also the vector-to-pixel rendering and animation features that make it an even more attractive standalone platform for pixel artists.
Pepakura
Pepakura is a paper craft designing platform. When I was in middle school, I was really into papercrafts because there were a lot of props and toys I could not afford, so I got used to building them myself! You can design new paper crafts using their tool, or use a viewer to check out other creations in the gallery and try to build them yourself!

Spline

A timesheets tool that lets you track how much time you spend on your projects for personal time management, team projects, or client reporting.

When you really need to (not) do the most. Don't let future you get impatient. Cold Turkey is the strictest blocker on sites and desktop apps so that you can make sure that your time spent on screen is spent doing exactly what you intended. Except I think I had a dispicable time testing random attributes when setting it up, so if you use it, make sure to start small.
Edo Agenda

Lanes

Programming and Development

Bulma
Just 1 CSS file to do it all. This open source CSS framework and bootstrap alternative that makes managing CSS a breeze while easily building HTML on top of it without needing JS. If you do want even more features though, you can pair it with Buefy which is based on Bulma and utilizes Vue.js.
Observable
Open source data exploration. Collaboratively get inspired and learn new ways of visualizing data across the world, including hot topics and art. Endless fun honestly.
Simple.css
As simple as it gets for CSS, a personal project by Kevin Quirk based on New.css.

Project Management and Creative Ideation

Airtable
Airtable is a workspace tool that that can also be used to build applications! They offer tons of templates from even planning to video production. Like many other workspace tools, you're getting a bunch of different views and integrations built right in.
ClickUp
Not your average product management tool â€” a competitor to Monday, Trello, and Wrike especially for its cross platform integration support which allows to curate the experience of ClickUp based on which team members you want to introduce it to. If your project management team is prepared to roll out the learning curve and training together, I definitely consider it a leader of its kind.
Google Jamboard
A collaborative whiteboard that is easy for everyone with a Google account to access instantly. While limited in features compared to many other collaborative whiteboard platforms, when you need to make a quick mural of sticky notes or have need a low-tech learning curve for your audience, it doesn't hurt to just get ready and jam.
Kinopio
A virtual mindmapping experience like you've never seen before, with some epic support for gifs, stickers, filters, and links. Get inspired by exploring spaces made by others or get started with a pre-made template.
Wrike
Another fan favorite for project management that works great for teams, though it's really meant for particularly larger teams compared to Monday.com which can be applied more easily to organizations both small and large.

Utilities

Anki
Intelligent multimedia flash cards based on spaced based reptition. Anki has become extremely popular over the years, with most space-based reptition features that are built today being built off of it.
Brella
For hosting virtual events, exhibitions, and conferences, with chat, video streaming, networking features, and more.
ClickDrop
Finally something useful made for AR. Quickly an image by taking a photo of your phone and drop that right into your computer, an idea that would make taking annotations from physical books a dream. You can try out a demo for free, but its full capabilities do come with a lofty price for now as there are no competitors for what they do â€” not unless you want to try building it yourself I guess.
Equity Maps
Manually track air time in a meeting or classroom and the flow of conversation. This interface allows you to easily create a map while folx in the room are speaking and then assess the results using equity analytics that take identity markers into consideration. The legitmacy of this approach and of imposing it on students might be a bit questionable â€” but it may ultimately be a necessary means of framing accountability in a space. This tool was used in some of my classes in high school, but there was no explanation of why the map was being made so I had no idea that there were analytics either. I only knew we were graded on the "balance" of the conversation, so I am not sure if an inequitable conversation was graded against those who were harmed by a space.
Here
Hyper-flexible video rooms like nothing you've ever seen before. A collaborative white board, digital canvas, and conferencing platform all in one. Even supports screenshare and drag and drop. Reimagine video calls while making the most of virtual space.
Kami
My go-to PDF editor back in high school â€” I did my physics PSETs on here for YEARS. This friendly and collaborative platform has immensely expanded its features for educational settings, supporting Google Classroom, Schoology, and Canvas, you can comment just as you would on Google Docs, and still get all the typical features of a normal PDF editing tool.
Kast
Kast bought out the remains of Rabb.it which allowed you to host a room and share content on a virtual computer. It was known as an ideal loophole watch Netflix with other people. Skirting on questionable means, it's no wonder they did not end up getting full funding. Kast remains a streaming service, though only from their list of partners and services. It still remains an interesting tool for hosting watch parties with support for built-in chat and video call, but it certainly will NEVER be what Rabb.it once was. For security reasons? That might be for the better, at least for now. Using the platform definitely made me reminisce of the updated versions of Rabb.it... which I hated, but somehow was even more difficult to use than I was used to, thus I did away with it in the end. If you can get all your friends on it, it's worth a try.
Kosmi
Kosmi appears to be a browser-based Rabb.it alternative, again supporting voice and video for the purposes of creating a "How do I watch shows with my friends who don't have Netflix?" loophole, assuming the person who has Netflix is hosting the streaming from their browser. Generally the case when trying to screenshare Netflix by accessing it from a separate app, you'll get a black screen. My guess is since it's built into a browser exension, similar to Netflix party, there's no reason to have the stream disrupted. Haven't tried it myself since I'm not much of a Netflix person.
Macro
Built on top of Zoom, Macro allows you to engage using different views, check out the airtime map of speakers, and manage meeting minutes â€” but it is currently exclusive to macOS.
Mendeley
When I first learned about Mendeley, I didn't have the skills or context to truly appreciate it, and thus I ended up on the Zotero path. Many of the core features make it a Zotero alternative. Where it lacks in open source support, mandatory cloud storage, snapshots and accruate metadata extraction, it glows in multi-platform app support and its personal catalogue of saved papers. If you're outside of institutional access to databases, this can be a really attractive resource.
Mmhmm
A video calling power up that includes features such as allowing you to project yourself on top of your slides, prepare additional materials around your meeting, and delegate facilitation tools effectively to your copilot.
NeuraCache
Where Anki meets notetaking: leverage space-based reptition into your knowedge bases with a method that's already proven to work, without having to go in and make each flashcard on your own. Get to know what you know well.
Otter
A personal must-have for meetings for those who can afford it. Otter's optimized transcription AI quickly makes annotable scripts of your captions changing the experience of being just one and done with meetings without being able to draw anything out of them or shutting people out. If it were up to me, every instituion would make so to make this free and available to students â€” no questions asked. Baseline accessibility to information through transcriptions should be built-in and maintains accountability, rather than leaving it as a last minute though about additional accomodations.
RemNote
A knowledge base notetaking tool with built in spaced-based reptition capacity. Like other knowledge base tools of its kind, it supports backlinks and portals, while also being easy to search and share. While the free version is personally not my cup of tea, I've seen some RemNote users make some enviable databases, thus still making it a worthy contender.
Riff
With an array of analytics, Riff is its own standalone video conferencing platform that tracks air time, influence, interruptions, and affirmations across attendees.
Roam Research
This knowledge base tool is a fan favorite for those of its kind, one of those reasons being the way they represent their grid of nodal netowrks. Roam notes are hosted on their own server with limitations on storage as a result. Still, if you're able to get access to it for free or at a discount through their Roam Scholars program, it's definitely worth a try.
Snap Camera
Wondering how people turned themselves into a potato on meetings? Snap Camera allows you to access a variety of classic AR filters from Snapchat to use on your desktop video call or while streaming. Unfortunately, it doesn't work on my computer, but is fun to see on others!
